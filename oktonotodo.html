PLACEMENTS.

Samhitha Bhat on May 15, 201

Today, I woke up and decided to skip college and just sit at home and do nothing. Don’t get me wrong, but doing NOTHING is a huge task. It’s as good as meditating. Have you ever thought of not doing anything? By that, I don’t mean browsing through your phone, sitting on the couch, mindlessly watching daytime TV. It means doing something that makes you feel relaxed, gives you mental space and slows down your heart rate.

A day where you can relax and think about nothing. Most of us are so hard on ourselves almost always. It’s very important to sleep those extra hours, take extra care of your body, maybe watch a movie at home. You could also include taking time to cook a meal from scratch, walking in nature or setting aside time for meditation as other positive ways to “do nothing.”

We spend so much time running around trying to look busy for other people, that we often feel like we have to look busy for ourselves, otherwise we might be considered lazy. We need to stop, slow down and just do nothing to get back in touch with our true selves.

There’s absolutely nothing wrong with doing nothing; it’s a belief cast on us by society, so you can just choose to ignore it if you want. While everyone else is following the herd and “looking busy,” you can blaze your own trail by implementing moments of relaxed nothingness and feel all the better for it.

For me, “doing nothing” is writing this blog. Oh, how I love doing nothing!.
